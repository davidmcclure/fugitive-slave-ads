Transcript: $100 REWARD!! RANAWAY from the Subscriber, on the 4th of September 1835, a negro man by the name of ABRAHAM CAMP: between 35 and 40 years of age, slim and spare made, not very black, and is about one-fourt part Indian blood; he has large feet according to his size, is about 5 feet, ten or eleven inches high, is a very good blacksmith, and plays the fife and fiddle both tolerably well.<BR>Said fellow may endeavor to pass himself as free, but if any body should apprehend him, and be doubtful, let them examine the scars inflicted on him for his misbehaviour.  He has a down look when interrogated, and his countenance is not very good.<BR>Any person who will apprehend & deliver said fellow to me, or confine him in some Jail so that I can get him, shall receive the above reward ($100,) if not wounded.  If he will not give up in reason, take him dead.  A reward of $10 will be given in this case.<BR>The Subscriber lives in Davidson county, N.C. near Lexington.<BR>JOSEPH WAGGONER.<BR>January 1, 1836    10 tf
