Headline: Ten dollars reward
Publishing newspaper: Wilmington, N.C. : People's Press
Date of advertisement: 1835-11-13
Page: 4
Time period (decade): 1830-1839
Person placing ad: Jere Nichols
Date of departure: 1835-10-13
Slave name(s): Rose
Slaveholder: L. Rivers
Transcript: Ten Dollars Reward.<BR>RAN away from the subscriber on the 13th day of October last, in Wilmington, a negro woman named<BR>ROSE.<BR>The said Rose is about 40 years of age, complexion black; Rose makes a very decent appearance for a negro and speaks very plain and plausible; said Rose runaway without any cause, but took a fright from a false report of her being or about to be sold to the speculators, by Mr. L. Rivers, from whom I hired her. I had no doubt but she would return, after word had been given out that there was no intention of any thing of the kind; and if she will now return, without forther[sic] trouble, she will not be disturbed.<BR>All persons are forewarned from harboring, concealing, or carrying away said ROSE--particularly masters of vessels.<BR>JERE NICHOLS.<BR>Nov. 13.<BR>[illegible]
