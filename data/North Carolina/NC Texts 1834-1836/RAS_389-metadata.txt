Transcript: $50 REWARD.<BR> RANAWAY from the Subscriber, in Rutherford County, North Carolina, on the 15th instant, a Negro Man named WARDY, aged about 30 years, quite black, heavy built, about 5 feet 8 or 10 inches high. On examination of his face, one or two scars will be discovered supposed to be occasioned by a bruise; he has a very large foot. When he eloped he had on a dark colored pair of pantaloons, a black hat, half worn; other clothing not recollected. In his countenance he is inclined to have rather a down cast. <BR> Also, a Negro Woman named LUCY, aged about 38 years, very black; her front teeth tolerably worn. She is spare made, and of common height; has a good countenance. She had on, when she eloped, a calico frock of light color.<BR> The above Negroes were purchased in Sampson County, North Carolina, (to wit:  Wardy of a man by the name of Hicks, and Lucy of a Mr. Ellington;) by Dr. Harrison Jordan, of Madison County, Mississippi. A reward of FIFTY DOLLARS will be given for the apprehension and securing in any Jail in this State, the above Negroes. Letters may be addressed to L. F. Henderson, or C.C. Henderson, at Lincolnton, each of whom is authorized to receive said Negroes from the apprehension.<BR> THOMAS COLE, Agent<BR>For HARRISON JORDAN.<BR> Lincolnton, N.C., August 22, 1835. 4t*
