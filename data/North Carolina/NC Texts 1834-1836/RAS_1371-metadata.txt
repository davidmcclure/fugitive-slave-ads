Transcript: Thirty Dollars Reward.<BR>RUNAWAY from the subsciber, living in Johnston county, 15 miles south-east of Raleigh, on the 1st of March, 1835, negro man named Peter, about 5 feet 8 or 10 inches high, about 26 years old, rather yellow complexion, a scar on the right hand, and has a very fierce look, and speaks quick when spoken to.<BR>Also, about the first of August last, negro woman Peggy, the wife of said Peter, 5 feet 6 or 8 inches high, dark complexion, and about 23 or 24 years old.<BR>It is supposed that they are harbored by negroes living between the subscriber's house and Raleigh; or very likely they may be lurking in the immediate vicinity of Raleigh, Peter having worked there some time previous to his running away, and no doubt formed many acquaintances.<BR>The sum of Twenty Dollars will be given for the apprehension of Peter, and Ten dollars for Peggy, if delivered to the subscriber in Johnston county, or confined in the Wake or Johnston county jail.<BR>HARDY JONES.<BR>Johnston, May 24, 1936    84
