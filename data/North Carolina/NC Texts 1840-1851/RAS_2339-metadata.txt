Headline: $60 REWARD
Publishing newspaper: Charlotte, N.C. : Charlotte Journal
Date of advertisement: 1849-02-02
Page: 3
Time period (decade): 1840-1849
Person placing ad: John M. Potts
County of residence: Mecklenburg County (N.C.)
Place of residence: White Hall, Steel Creek
Slave name(s): William; Norman; Dick
Former slaveholder: Asa Helms; H. C. Owens
Transcript: RANAWAY from the subscriber, living at White Hall, Steel Creek, 8 miles south-west of Charloote, three boys, viz: <br></br>WILLIAM<br></br>(known as Joseph H. Wilson's carriage driver;) he is about 33 years old, full 6 feet high, with one front tooth out, and very polite when spoken to.<br></br>NORMAN<br></br>is 26 years of age, purchased of the Administrators of H. C. Owens, dec'd.; has a scar on his left hand with two stiff fingers, yellow complected, about 5 feet 10 inches high, and eyes somewhat inflamed.<br></br>DICK<br></br>is about 28 years of age, about 6 feet high, and weighs about 212 pounds, dark complected, with a large full face, purchased of Asa Helms, of Union County.<br></br>As William and Norman as travelled in the mountains with their masters, they will no doubt attempt to pass that way and also endeavor to procure free papers.<br></br>The above reward will be given for the three boys above-mentioned, or $20 for each, on delivery, or their confinement in any Jail so that I may get them again.<br></br>JOHN M. POTTS<br></br>January 16, 1849.             14tf
