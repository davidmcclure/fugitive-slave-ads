Transcript: Twenty Dollars Reward.<BR>RAN away from the Subscriber, on the 7th instant, a negro man named<BR>MILES,<BR>twenty two years of age, about feet seven or eight inches high, well formed, and when spoken to has a downcast look, and a slight impediment in his speech. He has been accustomed to boating, and has been employed on board of lighters between this place and Ocracoke. Masters of vessles and all others are forwarned from harbouring, employing, or carrying him away, under the penalty of the law. The above reward will be paid for his delivery to me, or for securing him in jail in Newbern.<BR>SAMUEL E. SMITH<BR>Newbern, 25th July, 1840.
