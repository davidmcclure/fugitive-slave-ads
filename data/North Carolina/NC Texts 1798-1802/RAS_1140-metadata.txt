Date of advertisement: 1801-03-31
Page: 1
Time period (decade): 1800-1809
Person placing ad: William Mitchell; Wilson Jones
Presumed or intended destination: Nash County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Sion; Abraham
Slaveholder: William Mitchell;Wilson Jones
Transcript: Forty-five Dollars Reward.<BR>RUN AWAY, some Days ago, from the Subscribers, living in Person County, TWO NEGRO MEN, one named Sion, the other Abraham.  Sion is a yellow Fellow, about five Feet ten Inches high, has both his ears cropped, and has a smiling Countenance.  Abraham is a small balck fellow, about four Feet seven or eight Inches high : he has uncommonly small Eyes, and is not very sensible.<BR>These Negroes took with them a bright bay Horse, nearly five Feet high, five Years old this Spring; has a small Blate in his Face, neither docked nor branded, one or both of his hind Feet white.<BR>Sion having been raised in Nash County, it is supposed they would make for that County.<BR>Any person apprehending either of the said Negroes, and restores him to his Owner, will receive Twenty Dollars Reward ; or Forty-five Dollars for both the Negroes and the Horses.<BR>WM.[William] MITCHELL.<BR>WILSON JONES.<BR>March 25, 1801.
