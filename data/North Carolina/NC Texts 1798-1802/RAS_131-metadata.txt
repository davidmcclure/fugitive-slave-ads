Transcript: RAN-away from John Barclay (to whom he was hired) a negro fellow named<BR>JACK,<BR>Pretty stout made, of a yellow complection, a forbidding countenance, and about five feet eight inches high.<BR>It is impossible to say what dress he may appear in, as he has a variety of cloaths, and may change them occasionally. For the best part of these eighteen months past he has been openly harboured at Mr. John M'Kenzie's plantation, Brunswick county, (where he has been seen lately) and frequently at his kitchen in this town. A Reward of Five Dollars will be given for securing said negro so that I may get him again, and an adequate allowance made for distance and expence.<BR>JOHN BROWN.<BR>Wilmington August 7--f
