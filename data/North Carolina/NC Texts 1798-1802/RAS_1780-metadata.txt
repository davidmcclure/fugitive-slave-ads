Headline: Sixty dollars reward

Date of advertisement: 1801-10-15
Page: 3
Time period (decade): 1800-1809
Person placing ad: Benjamin Smith
Presumed or intended destination: Dollison's up the Northwest River
Slave name(s): Toney; Margaret; Nanny
Slaveholder: Benjamin Smith
Former slaveholder: Mr. Parviance
Transcript: Sixty Dollars Reward.<BR> Will be given by the subscriber for delivery to himself or his Overseer at Belvendere, TOBEY, MARGARET, and NANNY, who have absconded some months past--And for proving to conviction their being harbored by any white person. One third of the same for delivery and proof of each or either--For delivery alone Ten Dollars each, and five dolars each for proof of being harbored by a person of color.<BR> TONY, is a short well set fellow, about forty years of age, stutters, and was formerly well known as a boatman--He has been frequently about Dollison's up the North-west River, and I'm informed is very probably harboured by some of Major Moore's negroes. <BR>MARGARET, is an old wench of a yellowish complexion, has been hired about town for many years, usually stayed at Mr. Keddie's premises  and frequeneted the Sound very much.<BR>NANNY is you and small, formerly lived with Mrs. Parviance at the Sound, and is supposed to be harboured in the neighbourhood.<BR> BENJA. SMITH. <BR> October 8.
