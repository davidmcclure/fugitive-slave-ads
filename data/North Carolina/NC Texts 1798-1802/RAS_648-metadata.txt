Transcript: TWENTY DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RUN away from the subscriber on Wednesday the 26th inst. about twelve miles from Raleigh, on the road to Spruib' Ferry on Cape Fear, two Negroes, to wit: KATE, an uncommon likely Negro Woman, 19 or 20 years of age, long bushy hair, being of an Indian extraction, very filthy, her complexion rather bright, yet not much of a mulatto, branded when young on the left cheek I.H. had on a plain homespun cotton jacket and coat, dyed purple, shoes, stockings, hat and blanket.<BR>MYLES, a very likely boy, 12 or 14 years of age, well fed, thick lips, pert eyes, walks with his toes in, had on a negro cotton jacket and overalls, dyed purple, an oznabrigs shirt, shoes, stockings, hat and blanket.<BR>I expect the wench will endeavour to get to Norfolk, where I purchased her, and the boy to Halifax or Mush Island, where I purchased him of Col. James Tabb, or perhaps they may have been persuaded by some scoundrel to elope with him to the western country.  I will give the above reward to any person, or in proportion for either of them, who will deliver them to the goaler in Raleigh, Smithfield, or Halifax; or give such certain information to Benjamin Lockhart, of Wake, or John Lockhart or Northampton, as will lead to the recovery of them<BR>THOMAS LOCKHART.<BR>Raleigh, 28th Dec. 1798     38 3
