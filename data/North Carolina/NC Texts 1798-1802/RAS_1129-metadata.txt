Headline: Forty dollars reward
Publishing newspaper: Salisbury, N.C. : The North Carolina Mercury and Salisbury Advertiser
Date of advertisement: 1799-06-27
Page: 3
Time period (decade): 1790-1799
Person placing ad: James Baber
Date of departure: 1799-05-20
County of residence: Rutherford County (N.C.)
Presumed or intended destination: In possession of Joseph Tribble
Slave name(s): Moses; Jim
Slaveholder: James Holland
Transcript: FORTY DOLLARS REWARD <BR> RUNAWAY from the subscriber living in Rutherford County (N.C>) about the 20th of May last, two NEGRO MEN, one named Moses of yellow complexion about 5 feet 10 inchs high, and about 25 years of age, ther other named Jim, about 5 feet 8 inches high, and about 30 years of age.  The subscriber has reason to believe that said negroes are now in possession of a Joseph Tribble, who frequently passes for Colonel Tribble; and is a man of tolerable address, about 6 feet 2 icnhes high, and about 35 years of age, of a dark complexion,--Whoever will apprehend the above described negroes, and secure them so that the owner can get them shall receive the above reward, and the farther reward of fifty dollars if they apprehend said Tribble and confine him so that the operation of Law may be laid on him, for feloniously taking said negroes.<BR> JAMES BARBER.<BR> N.B. One of said negroes belongs to Major James Holland, in Rutherford county, near the Court-House.
