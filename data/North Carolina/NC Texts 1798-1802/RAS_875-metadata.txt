Transcript: TWENTY DOLLARS REWARD. <BR>. RUNAWAY from the subscriber on Sunday night on the 9th instant, a likely Negro fellow and Wench, by the names of Peter and Hager; Peter is a black fellow about six feet high, had on when he went away a green negro cotton jacket and a pair of homespun trowsers, dyed of purple colour, also, an old surtout coat of grey collour much worn. Hager is a likely wench of a yellow complexion, about 5 feet 8 or 10 inches high, her dress is chiefly of homespun cotton cloth. Any person who will apprehend said negroes and deliver them to me, or secure them so that I get them again, shall receive that above reward, or ten dollars for either of them. <BR> LAWRENCE MOORE. <BR> Little Brown-Creek, Anson county,}<BR>February 18, 1800. }
