Transcript: TWENTY DOLLARS REWARD,<BR>WILL be paid to any person who will deliver to me, or confine in any jail so that I get him, a Negro Man, by the name of SIMON, who ranaway from my Plantation, in Sampson County, on the night of the 7th inst. He is about 5 feet 11 inches high, aged, to appearance, about 40; very light complexion, blue eyes, hair inclined to be sandy coloured, somewhat bald headed, a down look, and on his left check is branded the letter M, which he will, no doubt, endeavour to conceal by a long beard. He is well acquainted about Wilmington and the Sound near there. All Masters of Vessels, and other persons, are forbid employing or harboring said fellow, under the penalty of the Law. His color being nearly white, may induce him to attempt to pass as a free man.<BR>WILEY MOBLEY.<BR>Clinton, Sept. 9, 1821.     tf.<BR>The Editor of the Newbern Centinel, will please insert the above Advertisement until forbid, and forward his account to this Office for payment.
