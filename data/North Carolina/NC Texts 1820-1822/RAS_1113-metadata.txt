Transcript: Fifty Dollars Reward.<BR>MADE his escape from me, on Friday evening the 4th of the present month, near Stantonsburg, a negro man named<BR>ALLEN,<BR>(calls himself Allen Woodard) he is about 30 years of age, of a tolerable size, yellow complexion, a pretty good House Carpenter and a very ingenious negro.  He formerly belonged to Wm.[William] Dickinson, decd.[deceased]--and has lately been confined in the Newbern gaol, was removed thence to Snow Hill, [illegible] his trial and was whipped--his back is pretty much scar[r]ed.  It is said he has forged free papers, with which he has passed as a free man.  It is probable he will be lurking about Newbern as he carried a white woman there, with whom he was intimate as it was said.<BR>The above reward will be given any person who will deliver him to me, or lodge him in Tarborough gaol.<BR>DANIEL DICKINSON.<BR>Edgcomb County, 2 miles above Stantonsburg, May 8th, 1822.}
