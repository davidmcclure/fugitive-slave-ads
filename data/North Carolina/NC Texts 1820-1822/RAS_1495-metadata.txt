Transcript: OUTLAWRY.<BR>STATE OF NORTH CAROLINA,<BR>New Hanover County<BR>M. W. CAMPBELL and JOHN MACCOLL, Esq'rs. two of the Justices of the Peace in and for said County.<BR>WHEREAS, complaint hath this day been made to two of the Justices of the Peace of the said county, ROBERT W. BROWN, of the said county, (merchant) that a certain male slave, belonging to him, named CUPID, hath absconded himself from his said master's service, and is lurking about in the said county, committing acts of felony and other misdeeds.<BR>These are therefore, in the name of the State, to command the said slave forthwith to surrender himself and return home to his said master--And we do hereby also require the Sheriff of the said county of New Hanover, to make diligent search and pursuit after the said slave; and him having found to apprehend and secure so that he may be conveyed to his said master, or otherwise discharged as the law directs--And the said Sheriff is hereby authorised and empowered, to raise and take with him such power of his county as he shall think fit, for apprehending the said slave. And we do hereby, by virtue of the act of Assembly in such case provided, intimate and declare, that if the said slave named CUPID, doth not surrender himself and retern[sic] home immediately after the publication of these presents, that any person may kill and destroy the said slave, by such means as he or they may think fit, without accusation or impeachment of any crime or offence for so doing, and without incurring any penalty and forfeiture thereby.<BR>Given under our hands and seals, the eleventh day of April in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and twenty-one.<BR>M. W. CAMPBELL, J. P.   (Seal)<BR>J. MACCOLL, J. P.    (Seal.)
