Former slaveholder: Nathan Smith Esq.[Esquire];David Smith;Samuel Bunting
Transcript: Twenty Dollars Reward,<BR>AND ALL REASONABLE EXPENSES PAID.<BR>ABSCONDED from the Subscriber on the Pee Dee, near the Long Bluff, on the 2d instant, two Negro Fellows, viz:<BR>BEN & BILL.<BR>BEN is supposed to be about 25 years of age, spare made and very tall and light complected.  I imagine he will make his way for Newbern or Wilmington as he was purchased of Nathan Smith, Esq. of the former place.<BR>BILL is near the same height, about 22 years of age, dark complected, thick lips, and is inclined to keep his mouth open--his left foot turns out.  He was purchased from David Smith of Wilminton[sic], and formerly the property of Samuel Bunting of New-Hanover and it is supposed he will make his way either to Newbern or Wilmington.<BR>Masters of vessels and others are warned from harboring either of the said fellows, as the law will be rigedly[sic] enforced.<BR>JOHN B. HERRINGTON.<BR>Letters addressed to me at Sampson Court-House, giving any information of either of the said Negroes will be thankfully received.   J.B.H.<BR>June 5, 1821--169 3w.
