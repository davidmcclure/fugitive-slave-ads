Transcript: $50 REWARD.<BR>RAN AWAY from the subscriber on the 23d inst. while on a visit at the Shocco[?] Spring, a Mulatto Man, named ALFORD, (calls himself Alford Williams.) He is about 25 or 26 years of age, 5 feet 5 or 6 inches high; has a large mouth, with a hairy mole on the upper lip, and slow lazy walk.  He took with him a variety of clothes; among which were, a blue cloth coat, blue gingham short jacket, yellow striped homespun pantaloons, a fur hat, with coarse oil on cloth cover, and a brown great coat.  He can read and write, and sing Psalms, &c. by note, and pretends to be very religious.  I will give the above reward for his delivery to me in Newbern, or twenty-five dollars, if he be secured in any jail so that I get him again.<BR>All persons and masters of vessels particularly, are forwarned from harboring or employing him, under the penalty of the law.<BR>E. PASTEUR.<BR>Shocco Spring, Warren c'y[county],<BR>Sept 24, 1821    49 tf<BR>N.B. He has taken with him one of my pistols,  a well finished iron barrel, eight square, and smooth bore.
