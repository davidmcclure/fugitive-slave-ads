Transcript: Ten dollars Reward<BR>RANAWAY from the Subscriber on the 16th of January last, my negro man<BR>TOM,<BR>(commonly called Tom Walker)-He is thirty five years of age, midling[sic] black, spare made about 5 feet 10 or 11 inches high, very talkative and impudent; he is branded with the letter T on each cheek; he has a scar on one of his cheeks and a scar on the end of his nose, occasioned by the fall from a house; a Blacksmith by trade, and a noted fiddler<BR>ALSO,<BR>My negro man named SIMON a mulatto, 22 years old. 5 feet 4 or 5 inches high, spare made, humble and inoffensive; he wore away a homespun surtout coat of a dark colour, which is very streeked[sic] in the fitting; also double wove cotton jacket and trowsers of a smoak[sic] colour, and white cotton socks--He is well known in Washington and its neighbourhood, where I expect he is lurking. I will give the above reward for each of them if lodged in the Jail of this county, & $20 if lodged in any Jail out of the county so I get them again. All persons, and masters of vessels particuarly are cautioned from harbouring or carrying them out of the State, under the penalty of the Law.<BR>THOMAS TROTTER.<BR>Beaufort County, Feb. 8, 1821--t294
