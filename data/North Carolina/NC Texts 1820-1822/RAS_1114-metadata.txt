Transcript: Fifty dollars Reward.<BR>RAN AWAY about the 1st of February last, my negro man BILL, about 28[?] years old 5 feet 8 or 10 inches high, very black complexion, and a little bow legged--he is probably lurking about Washington, where he is well known--<BR>And about the 10th of June, a negro man named GEORGE, about 24 years old. 5 feet 8 or 10 inches high, very black complexion, and has remarkably large white eyes. George was formerly the property of Doct. Thomas A. Cabarrus, subsequently the propertly of Mr. Benjamin Ranyon, and recently purchased by me from him. George is probably lurking about Edeonton, where he was raised, and I understand, has a sister who was emancipated by the late A. Cabarrus.<BR>The above reward will be paid for the apprehension of and delivery of the above negroes, or $25 for either of them if delivered to me, or secured in any Jail, so that I get them again.     JOHN MYERS.<BR>Bellelent[?], near Washington,<BR>Beaufort County, 20th Nov 1820--tf287
