Slave name(s): Hammond
Slaveholder: William L. Bush
Transcript: RAN AWAY<BR>FROM the Subscriber, on the 20th instant, a negro man named HAMMOND, aged about 28 years, 5 feet 10 or 11 inches high, dark complexion, ann[sic] has a scar on his breast; whed[sic] spoken to has a down look; he can read and write, and I expect will write himself a pass; he generally carries a book about him.  Had on when he went away, a long brown surtout coat, and blue cloth pantaloons.  I expect he is about Wilmington, and may make for Newbern or Fayetteville.<BR>I will give a reward of TWENTY DOLLARS for his delivery to me, near Wilmington, or to any jailor in the state, and all reasonable expences paid.  Masters of vessels are strictly forbid harboring or carrying away said negro, as, upon conviction, the penalty of the law will be indiscriminately enforced.<BR>WILLIAM L. BUSH.<BR>February 24--156 4w.
