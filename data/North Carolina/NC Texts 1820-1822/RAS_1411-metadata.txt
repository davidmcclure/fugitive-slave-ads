Time period (decade): 1820-1829

Slave name(s): Rose
Slaveholder: Christopher Dawson
Transcript: Ten Dollars Reward.<BR>RANAWAY from the Subscriber, on Friday morning last, a Negro Woman named ROSE.  About 23 years old, of the common stature, black complexion had on when she went away, a black striped domestic cotton frock.  ROSE is very well known in Newbern, and had lived their[sic] for about 18 years antecedent to my taking her to my plantation whence she made her escape.<BR>It is supposed that she is lurking about Newbern and its vicinity, as she has a mother at Mr. John Coart's, and might easily be apprehended by the partrolers or watchmen of the town.<BR>The above reward will be paid for the apprehension and delivery of said Negro at my plantation, or to secure her in Newbern Jail so that I get her again.<BR>I hereby forwarn all persons from harboring or employing said Negro, and particularly master's of vessels from carrying her away as I am determined to enforce the law against all offenders without distinction.<BR>CHRISTOPHER DAWSON.<BR>Dawson's Creek, Craven <BR>County, June 6th, 1821,   169 4w.
