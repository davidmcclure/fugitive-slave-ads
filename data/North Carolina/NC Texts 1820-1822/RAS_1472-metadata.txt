Transcript: 100 Dollars Reward<BR>RAN AWAY from the Subrciber[sic] on the 29th instant, a Negro Man named<BR>ANTHONY,<BR>about five feet seven or eight inches high, and is twenty-five or twenty-six years of age ;--of a dark complexion, very active and ingenious, and is quite talkative.  Had on when he went away, white homespun shirt, trowsers filled in with black wool, wool hat, much worn ;--stole and carried with him from me, some good clothes, among them are a deep blue surtout coat, a light coloured close bodied coat, and a black silk vest.  ANTHONY has worked at the Blacksmith business for some time past, and pretends to possess a competent knowledge of the trade.  He is a cunning, artful fellow, is well dressed, and has no doubt obtained forged free papers and will probably attempt to go to some of the northern states.<BR>Masters of vessels and other are cautioned from harbouring or carrying away said negro, under the penalty prescribed by law.<BR>Any person apprehending said fellow within this state, and confining him in any jail so that I get him, shall receive a reward of Forty Dollars, and if he is taken up beyond the limits of this state, and properly secured, I will give a reward of One Hundred Dollars.<BR>EBENEZER BURNAP.<BR>Onslow County, N.C.<BR>January 30, 1821.     150 4w.
