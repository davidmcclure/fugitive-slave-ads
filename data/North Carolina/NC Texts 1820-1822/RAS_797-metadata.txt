Transcript: 50 DOLLARS<BR>REWARD.<BR>RANAWAY from the Subscriber, on the 12th of February last a Negro Man named<BR>NAT;<BR>He is about five feet seven inches high, quite black, has a large flat nose, very small ears, is stout made and stutters very badly, was raised on Tar River, by Benjamin Back, and sold by him to Weeks Chapman, from whom the Subscriber puchased him; he has acquaintances on Neuse River, Swift Creek, and Cotentnea[Contentnea], at one of which places he is probably lurking.  The last that was heard of him, he was in the neighborhood of Cotentnea[Contentnea], Greene County.<BR>All persons are hereby forwarned from harboring or employing him, under the penalty of the law.  The above Reward, and all reasonable expences will be paid to any person that will apprehend and deliver said runaway to the Subscriber, or secure him so that she gets him again.<BR>Sarah Chapman<BR>Swift Creek, Craven<BR>County, Nov. 11, 1820.     139tf
