Transcript: Ten Dollars Reward.<BR>RANAWAY<BR>MY man WILLIS lately purchased of Sheriff Black; about 5 feet 10 inches high, stout made, from 24 to 26 years of age; had on, when he went away, a suit of dark broad cloth, and large outside great coat.  Willis is a brown coloured mulatto, his feet inclined to be crooked, with risings or bumps on his face, resembling Small Pox, produced by his having been vaccinated, and a scar on the side of his mouth.  I will give the above reward for his apprehension and confinement in jail; or on his delivery to me in Fayetteville.<BR>C. KIRK.<BR>January 29, 1825--99-2t<BR>N.B. Willis has been seen, and is supposed to be now lurking on the plantation lately occupied by Mr. Black, about 2 miles from town, on the Hillsborough road.
