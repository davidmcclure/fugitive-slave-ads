Transcript: Fifty Dollars Reward.<BR>RANAWAY from the Subscriber on the 15th of March last, a negro man named BEN--about 25 years of age, of a yellow complection, has had his right thigh broke, which causes him to limp, and has a lump on the wound.  He is supposed to be lurking about the plantation of Smauel Street; or at Sarah Chapman's, on Swift Creek, where he has connexions; or in the neighbourhood of Major Pugh, in Pitt County.  The above reward and all reasonable expenses will be paid, on delivering said fellow to me, or securing him in any Jail so that I get him again.  All persons are forwarned from harboring, employing, or carrying him away under the penalty of the law.<BR>SETH CHAPMAN.<BR>April 4th, 1825--'68.
