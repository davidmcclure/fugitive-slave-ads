Headline: $20 reward
Publishing newspaper: Greensborough, N.C. : Carolina Patriot
Date of advertisement: 1838-04-13
Page: 3
Time period (decade): 1830-1839
Person placing ad: J.A. Foulkes
County of residence: Guilford County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Thornton
Slaveholder: J.A. Foulkes
Transcript: $20 REWARD.<BR>RANAWAY from the subscriber, in Guilford, on Saturday, the 31st ult., a negro man named Thornton, about 5 feet 8 or 9 inches high, and of a bright-yellow complexion; a small scar on one of his cheeks, not recollected--between 26 and 28 years of age, a miller by profession, carried off several suits of clothes; and as he eloped without provocation, it is not unlikely will try to leave the country, particularly if he should procure the ordinary facilities.<BR>I will give the above reward if taken in the limits of the State, and secured in any jail so that I get him again, or delivered to me, and all reasonable charges will be paid, and if out of this State fifty dollars.<BR>J.A. FOULKES.<BR>April 6, 1838.<BR>7.3t.
