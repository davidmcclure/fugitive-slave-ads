Transcript: RUNAWAY, <BR>FROM the subscriber about the 1st of March, a negro man named BOB, about five feet high, stout-built, quite black, about twenty-seven years old and formerly owned by Richard Fonveille, sen'r. He is supposed to be lurking in the vicinity of Newbern or about the plantation of Thomas J. Pasteur, at Green Spring, about a mile below the town, where he has a wife. I will give fifteen dollars reward for his recapture, if I obtain possession of him, or if lodged in the jail of this county. <BR>GRAVES FONVEILLE.<BR>March 22, 2837.[1837]   13tf
