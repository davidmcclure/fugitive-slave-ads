Transcript: $100 REWARD.<BR>RANAWAY from the subscriber a year past, negro man <BR>HARRY<BR>He is a bright black, about five feet 16[?] inches high, thirty years old; wears whiskers, has and[an] impediment in his speech, and a down look when spoken to. He is by profession a fiddler; a ditcher and sawyer. I purchased him in Halifax, at sheriff's sale, several years past, sold as the property of Wilson Carter. he has lately been seen in Bertie county, employed both as a ditcher and sawyer. He there passed as a freeman. I will give the above reward of one hundred dollars to any person who will deliver him to the jailor in Halifax, by the 15th October or fifty dollars, if delivered to him any time thereafter.<BR>B.C. EATON.<BR>Sept. 1, 1829.<BR>30--3w
