Time period (decade): 1820-1829
Person placing ad: Hamilton W. Cotter
Date of departure: 1829-05-15
County of residence: Pasquotank County (N.C.)
Presumed or intended destination: Perquimans County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Edmund
Slaveholder: Hamilton W. Cotter
Transcript: 25 Dollars Reward<BR>WILL be paid for the apprehension and delivery to me or confinement in jail so I get him again, negro boy<BR>EDMUND,<BR>Who run away from me on the 15th ult. He is 25 or 26 years old, 5 feet 8 or 10 inches high, very black; has lost a front tooth, and has a scar on the outside of one of his feet, believed to be the right. It is believed he is lurking about the neighborhoods of Mr. Joshua Skinners, in Perquimans, or Mr. John Winslows in the county, at both of which places he has a sister. I hereby forewarn all persons from harboring or employing him, and all masters of vessels from carrying him off, as the law will rigidly be enforced against all so offending.<BR>Hamilton W. Cotter.<BR>Newbegging Creek, Pasquotank County, June 1.
