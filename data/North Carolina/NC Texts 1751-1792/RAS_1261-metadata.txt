Date of departure: 1791-08-17
County of residence: Jones  County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): James
Slaveholder: Aaron Lambert
Transcript: Five Pounds Reward.<BR> RAN away from the subscriber on the 17th of August last, a likely young negro man named JAMES, about 21 years of age, five feet 7, or 8 inches high, of a yellowish complexion, by trade a blacksmith; most used to making axes, he stoops forward in his knees and body as if he as a burden on his back--his back is marked with the whip, he has a scar on one of his elbows occasioned by a burn; has small legs and very large feet--has a bold look, speaks good English and can tell a smooth tale--born on Trent river.--He had on and took with him the following clothes, viz. one blue great coat dyed in the cloth and turned whitish where it is worn, middling fine cloth, also a tight bodied coat, with holes at the elbows and under the arms, and a rent in shoulder; also a sky blue homespun do. and a jacket of nearly the same---also a homespun white linen jacket, a pair of corduroy, and a pair of buckskin breeches; new last fall, but worn in the crotch, two pair of trowsers, two shirts and fine new white shirt (6s. linen) stock and stock buckle, a pair of hold shoes and a pair of of sky coloured stockings--a new wool hat with purple ribbon round it and an older rackoon one.<BR>Whoever will take up said negro and return him to me, or secure him in any gaol so that I get him again, shall have the above reward and all reasonable charges paid by.<BR>AARON LAMBERT.<BR>N.B. All masters of vessels  and others are forwarned of harbouring, or carrying him off at their peril.<BR>Jones County, September 20.
