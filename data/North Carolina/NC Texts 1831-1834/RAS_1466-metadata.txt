Transcript: 100 Dollars Reward.<BR>RAN AWAY from the subscribers, on Monday morning, the 10th inst., negroes JOHN and ANDERSON. John is a bright mulatto, twenty-one years of age, born and raised at Washington, North Carolina, and formerly belonged to Dr. Willis L. Woodley; had when he left, a blue broad cloth coat, one pair cassinet pantaloons nearly new, one pair green cotton do. somewhat worn, and black fur hat, half worn; John is about five feet, eight or nine inches high, with black eyes and full head of hair, is well formed and tolerably stout. Anderson is also a mulatto, 23 years of age--had when he left a blue broad cloth coat, one pair of light blue pantaloons, one pair of gray do. a pair of new shoes and fur hat pretty much worn. He is about five feet eight or nine inches high, has a full head of hair, large feet, and is well formed. We will give the above reward of $100 for their apprehension and confinement in jail, either in this or any other State; so that we get them again, or fifty dollars for the apprehension and confinement of either of them, in any State, so that we get them again.<BR>THOMAS P. LITTLE,<BR>GEORGE LITTLE.<BR>Hertford County, N. C., March 14, 1834. 22[illegible]kt.
