Transcript: NOTICE.<BR>RUNAWAY from the subscriber on the 13th inst., a bright mulatto man, named <BR> GEORGE,<BR> who is remarkably artful and will probably endeavor to pass as a free man. He had on when he left two suits of fine homespun Jeans, one black filled with wool, the other nearly a pale scuff[?] color. He is about thirty years of age, about six feet high, inclinded to be knockkneed, flat footed, his toes turn out, much more than common,--his voice strong, and bold countenance. I will give a reward of twenty five dollars to any person who will apprehend said boy, & confine him in a any Jail out of the county, so that I will be enabled to get him again, any intelligence respecting the said boy will be thankfully received by me, directed to Pocket Creek Post Office, Moore County, N.C.<BR> DUNCAN MURCHISON.<BR> September 13, 1833.   6t99
