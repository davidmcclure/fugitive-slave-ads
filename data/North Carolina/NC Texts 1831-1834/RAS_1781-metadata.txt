Transcript: Sixty Dollars Reward.<BR>Ran away or stolen from the subscriber, about the last of August or the first of September, two negro boys, JIM and CALVIN. Jim is about 18 years of age, 6 feet and the rise high, weights 180 or 190 lbs, large full eyes, round shoulders, knock kneed, large hands and feet, a large bulge in the hollow of his feet, stutters when spoken to, black complexion.<BR>Calvin is about 20 years of age, yellow complexion, ring eyed, broad about his eye-brows, broad mouth, thin lips, punch mouth, slender made, supposed to weigh 130 or 140 lbs, the back of one of his hands much seared by a Cotton Gin, which draws some of his fingers, a large scar on his rump, caused by a burn.<BR>It is thought that these negroes have been conveyed off by some person or persons to the South or Western States. I will give 20 dollars for the apprehension of the negro boys, or 10 dollars for either of them, and 40 dollars for the conviction of the thief.<BR>I will thank any person for any information they can give relative to said negroes, by letters, directed to Buckhorn Falls, Chatham county, N. Carolina.<BR>JAMES BATTLE.<BR>Nov. 12, 1833<BR>48 6w
