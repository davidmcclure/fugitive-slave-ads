Transcript: RANAWAY<BR>ON the 5th instant, from the Subscriber, living in Fayetteville, a negro man named<BR>DANIEL,<BR>Aged about 33 years, 5 feet, 8 or 9 inches high, tolerably spare made, large mouth, a tooth out of the left upper jaw, which shows quite plain on opening his mouth, talks quite large, rather polite, wears his pantaloons large at the bottom. He was purchased of Malcom Buie of Moore County, in 1827, to whose neighborhood he has probably gone. He sometimes goes by the name of Daniel Buie. Whoever will take up said negro and return him to me, or lodge him in any jail, so that I get him again, shall be liberally rewarded.<BR>CONSTANT JOHNSON.<BR>June 10, 1833.<BR>26tf.
