Transcript: $10 REWARD.<BR>RUNAWAY from the Subscriber, on the 1st of December last, a Negro man named Charles, he is a dark Mulatto, between 30 and 35 years of age, about 5 feet 2 or 3 inches high, has a good countenance, inclining to laugh when spo[illegible] lowest[?] good humor, he is well set and stout to his [illegible] taken[?] visible mark except 3 or 4 of his teeth are out, 2 in front. His clothing is not particuarly recollected. Charles had a wife at the house of Mr. Thos.[Thomas] Davis of Cumberland, on big Rockfish, whose negroes are now in Richmond County, it is supposed he is lurking about Davis' and John Ferley's of Richmond county, who are in possession of said negroes at this time.<BR>DUNCAN McNEILL.<BR>Robeson County, Jan. 4, 1832.--1t.--p.
