Transcript: $20 REWARD.<BR> Ran away from the Subscriber, residing near Gen. Graham's Furnace, in Lincoln COunty, on the night of the 12th inst.,<BR> Three Negro Boys,<BR> Belonging to William Boylan, of Raleigh, N.C. viz: Ben--rather yellow complected, about 5 feet 10 inches high, 21 years of age.<BR> Jim--very black, and about the same height and age of Ben.<BR> Jerry--knock-need, very black, of a grum sulky look, and about the same age as the others.<BR> They were under my care, on their way to Mississippi. The above reward of $20 will be paid to any person who will apprehend and lodge the said Negroes in any jail in this State; or a proportionable part for either of them. It is probable that they will attempt to get back to the neighborhood of Raleigh.<BR>Any person apprehending the said Negroes, or any of them, will please to write to Mr. Wm.[William] Boylan, of Raleigh, by whom the Negroes will be received, and the reward paid.<BR> WILLIS SCOTT.<BR> Lincoln Co., Jan. 20, 1834.
