Transcript: 25 DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RAN away from the subscriber on the 19th of October last, a negro man named TONEY, five feet 8 or 10 inches high, of a black complexion, and smooth skin, and can dress decently if he will. He was sent to Mr. Gildersleeve's with a note, on business, and it is understood that he took up the idea that he was going to be sold or sent way, which was not so. Toney is well known in Craven County as a first rate hewer and timber-getter, and he can do pretty good work in a ship-year. He was raised on Bay River, and formerly belonged to Elijah Cratch, Esq. [Esquire], decd [deceased]. The above reward will be given for his delivery to the subscriber in Newbern, or for securing him in any jail, so that he shall get him.<BR>ELIJAH CLARK.<BR>Newbern, 7th November, 1833.
