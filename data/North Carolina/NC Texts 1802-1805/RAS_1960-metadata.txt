Transcript: Ten Dollars Reward.<BR>RAN away from the subscriber's Mills, near Lumberton, on the 3d December, 1804--two negroes, ARGYLL and his wife BRUNETTA--Argyll is a well made fellow, about 5 feet 7 or 8 inches high, 35 years old, yellow complexion, and very much pitted with the small-pox, by which he lost one eye. Brunetta is a very large wench, a little pitted with the small-pox, and very black complexion; she is well known in the town of Wilmington and Fayetteville, being once the property of Mr. S. Shaw, of or near Wilmington.<BR>The above reward wil be paid to any person apprehending them and lodging them in any goal in North-Carolina, so that I get them--or One Hundred Dollars on proof of their being harboured by any white person. Masters of vessels are cautioned not to take them away or employ them, under the penalty of law. <BR>Wm. Norment.<BR>Melville, January 8, 1805.
