Headline: Twenty dollars reward
Publishing newspaper: Halifax, N.C. : North Carolina Journal
Date of advertisement: 1804-06-04
Page: 4
Time period (decade): 1800-1809
Person placing ad: William Cain
Slave name(s): Jim
Slaveholder: William Cain
Transcript: Twenty Dollars Reward.<BR>RUN-AWAY from the subscriber on the 12th instant, eight miles from Hillsborough, a remarkable likely negro fellow named Jim, about thirty years old, about five feet 10 or 11 inches high; he is a tolerable shoemaker, stutters when he talks, he is of a yellow complexion, country born; had on and carried away with him, a store slipon cloth coat, a half wore hat, close bodied coat, waistcoat, and short breeches of the same mixed cotten and woll, of a dark colour, two pair of overalls, a pair blue store cloth: a black silk waistcoat, shorn greyish hunting shirt, with several other clothes which I can't describe.  The above reward shall be paid, with all reasonable expences, upon delivering the said fellow to me at  home, or if put in Hillsborough jail.<BR>WILLIAM CAIN.<BR>May 19th, 1804    19---5p
