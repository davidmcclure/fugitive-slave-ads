Transcript: Ten Dollars Reward.<BR>RAN-AWAY from Mr. Sampronius Rufs in Smithville, on the 27th of Novemeber, a Negro Girl, about 16 or 17 years of age, of a yellowish complection[sic] named HANNAH, she went up the river with some negroes of General Smith in a boat for his plantation, to Wilmington; she will probably embrace the first opportunity of going to Charleston; all masters of vessels will avoid carrying her off. The above reward will be paid for securing her in Wilmington Jail, and giving the subscriber notice, or fifteen dollars if delivered at his plantation on Charlotte, Brunswick County.<BR>JOHN GAUSE, Jun.<BR>December 5, 1803.--[illegible]--
