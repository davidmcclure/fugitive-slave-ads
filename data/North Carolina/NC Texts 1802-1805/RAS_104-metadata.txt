Transcript: RAN AWAY<BR>FROM the Subscriber living in Wilkesborough, on Monday the 10th inst. a likely MULATTO WOMAN named SUKEY, about twenty or twenty-one Years of Age, remarkably well made, tolerably dark complexion, and when she walks steps remarkably short. Had on when she went away nothing but Shift and Petticoat; took two Indian Blankets, and had either seven or eight dollars in Money.<BR>Any Person or Persons that will take up said Mulatto Woman, and bring her home or lodge her in some Goal, so that I get her again, shall be handsomely rewarded and all necessary Charges paid by me.<BR>JAMES PATTON.<BR>Wilkesborough, Sept. 25, 1804
