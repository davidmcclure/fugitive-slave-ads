Time period (decade): 1800-1809

County of residence: Guilford County (N.C.)
Presumed or intended destination: Washington (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Moses
Slaveholder: William M'Adow
Transcript: RANAWAY from the Subscriber on the 18th inst. living in Guilford County, a likely young negro Fellow, of a dark Complexion, about five Feet ten Inches high, round stooping Shoulders, a small Leg, a long broad flat Foot; has a handsome Face, but a narrow Chin; is about 24 years of age, and called Moses.? He came from the state of Maryland, where he followed the Water with his Master.? He has sailed round to Norfolk and Washington in this state.? He is a pleasant, well spoken Fellow, and delights in going on the water.? Since he left me I have learn that his intention was to trry[sic] to get to Washington or some of the seaport towns in this state.? He will probably pass for a free man.? He took away with him a good castor Hat, a cotton Shirt, a pair of Pantaloons, striped with a large stripe of white and a small one of blue, and a pair of sharp toed shoes.<BR>Any person whowill[sic] take up said Negro & secure him, so that I get him again, shall have ten Dollars reward : or if they bring him to me, I will make them a reasonable satisfaction.?I forwarn any Captain or Master of a Vessel from takin him, concealing or running off with said Negro Man under the Penalty of what the Law directs.<BR>WILLIAM M'ADOW.<BR>May 25th.
