Transcript: Ten Dollars Reward.<BR>RANAWAY from the Subscriber, living in Montgomery county, N.C., on the 3d inst. a negro man named JOHN, sometimes called Jack, about 22 years old, about 6 feet high, a little lame in one of his ancles, which may be discovered by close observation.  Took with him a small bundle of clothes, among which was a black bombazet frock coat.  He can read and write, and took with him a Testament, a Spelling Book, Scott's Lessons, Watt's Hymn Book, and a printed Note Book, with Inkstand and blank paper.  It has been told by another negro that he intended to go on to Fayetteville, and take shipping for a free State.  He wrote a pass for himself.<BR>The above reward will be paid for his apprehension and delivery to me, or confinement in Jail with information to me so that I get him again.  Letters to be directed to Jas.[James] M. Butler's Post Office, Montgomery county, N.C.<BR>DAVID BRUTON.<BR>March 16, 1828.--63-3tpd
