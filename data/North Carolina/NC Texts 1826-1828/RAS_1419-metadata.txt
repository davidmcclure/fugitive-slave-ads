Transcript: TEN DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RANAWAY from the subscriber, on the 5th inst. a negro man named <BR>BRISTER. <BR>He is about 26 years of age--black complexion--5 feet 6 inches high, and has large eyes. Had on, when he went away, a pair of drab pantaloons, an old fur hat, and a round jacket. He formerly belonged to the estate of the late John Doughty, and ranaway and went to John Smaw's in Newbern, who delivered him to the subscriber--agent for the Executrix of John Doughty.--It is supposed that he is lurking about Newbern or in its vicinity. <BR>The above reward will be given to any person who will secure said negro in Craven county Jail, or 15 dollars if secured in any other Jail in this State, so that I get him. <BR>I hereby forwarn all persons from harboring, employing, or carrying him away, under the penalty of the law. <BR>ALVA D. WARREN, <BR>Agent for Mrs. Hannah Doughty. <BR>Beaufort County, April 5. 1827.--'72 75pd
