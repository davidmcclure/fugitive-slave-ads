Transcript: Ten Dollars Reward.<BR>RANAWAY from the Subscriber, living in Anson County, on the 26th of December, 1827, EPHRAIM and PHILLIS his wife.  Ephraim was raised in or near Norfolk, Va., and Phillis in Johnson County, N.C. They will probably endeavor to get to one or the other of these places.  Ephraim is a stout boy of about 27 years of age.  Phillis is about 40 years of age, very fleshy and stout, quick spoken when attacked by any person.  They took from home two or three suits of clothes.  The boy had on when he left home, a dark colored casinet coat and a pair of mixt pantaloons.  The girl wore a checked homespun frock.<BR>The above reward and all reasonable expenses will be paid to any person who will deliver the said Negroes to me near Morven, in Anson county, or confine them in any jail in the counties of Anson, Richmond, Moore, Cumberland, or Wake.<BR>B.R. BAILEY.<BR>January 3, 1828.    52-5tp.
