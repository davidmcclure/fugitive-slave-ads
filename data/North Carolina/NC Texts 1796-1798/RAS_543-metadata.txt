Headline: 20 dollars reward
Publishing newspaper: Edenton, N.C. : State Gazette of North Carolina
Date of advertisement: 1798-08-08
Page: 4
Time period (decade): 1790-1799
Person placing ad: Shadrach Collins
Presumed or intended destination: Perquimans County (N.C.); Edenton (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Tom
Slaveholder: Shadrach Collins
Transcript: 20 DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RUN AWAY from the subscriber, a negro fellow named TOM, about five feet eight or ten inches high, about 25 years of age, very black; has a scar on his right eye, and has lost one or two of his eye teeth.  The fellow is well known in Edenton, and has a Mother living there.--The above reward will be given, if delivered to me at Pollok's Ferry, or one half of the money if he is confined in any jail so that I get him again, or to Thomas Satterfield in Edenton.--All masters of vessels are forwarned from harbouring said fellow, or carrying him away.<BR>SHADRACH COLLINS.<BR>July 29th, 1798.<BR>N.B> It is supposed he is lurking about Mr. William Littlejohn's quarter, in Duren's Neck, Perquimans county.
