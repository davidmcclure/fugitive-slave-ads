Publishing newspaper: Fayetteville, N.C. : The North Carolina Minerva and Fayetteville Advertiser
Date of advertisement: 1796-09-10
Page: 3
Time period (decade): 1790-1799
Person placing ad: Murdoch Minnes
Date of departure: 1796-08-13
County of residence: Robeson County (N.C.)
Presumed or intended destination: New Bern (N.C.);Norfolk (V.A.)
Slave name(s): Bill
Slaveholder: Murdoch M'innes
Transcript: TEN DOLLARS REWARD.<BR> RUN-AWAY from the subscriber on the 13th Aug. living in Robeson county, in the fork of Shoeheel, a negro man, about 25 years old, named BILL, about 5 feet 7 inches high, hard featured, rather of a yellow complection has some scars on his back, and his large toes is longer by half an inch than any of his other toes-had on when went away, a light coloured cassimere vest, a long brown cloth coat, with large covered buttons, and a pair of home-spun trowsers[sic]. I suppose he intends for Newbern or Norfolk. Any person who will secure him in any jail, or deliver him to Daniel Ray in Fayetteville, will receive the above reward.<BR> MURDOCH M'INNES.<BR> August 26.
