Slave name(s): Derry

Transcript: FIVE DOLLARS REWARD,<BR>RUN AWAY from the subscriber, living in Orange county.  on the 7th inst. a Negro man named DERRY : about twenty-seven years old, born in this country, dark complection[sic], about five feet eight or ten inches high : he has a place on the top of his head (a little to the right side, a bit larger than a dollar) that has no wool on, and appears white and scaly ; he commonly wears a handkerchief on his head to hide said place.  He has sundry clothes with him but cannot be described, as he is often trading.  Perhaps he will endeavour for Capefear[sic] river, near Wilmington, as he says he hath frieds[sic] there.  Who ever will take up said negro, and eliver[sic] him to me or put him in jail. so that I may ge[sic] him again shall receive the above reward, and reaonable[sic] expences paid by<BR>WILLIAM MEBANE<BR>Orange county, Feb. 22 1797   42 3t
