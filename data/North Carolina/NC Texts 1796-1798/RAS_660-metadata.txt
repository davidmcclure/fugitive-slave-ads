Slaveholder: Robert Freeman

Transcript: TWENTY DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RUN away, on Tuesday, the 13th inst. from my plantation, three miles from Warrenton, a negro man named JUBLOE, formerly the property of Thomas Alston, of this county.  He is about 45 or 50 years old, stout and well made, yellow complexion, and noted for his art and cunning; had on a homespun cotton shirt, blue vest and small cloths, and may have other clothes not recollected.<BR>Also a negro girl named ESTHER, about 19 years old, who the above described fellow prevailed to go with him.  She is black complected, low, and square made, and carries the marks of a whipping she received the day before her departure.<BR>Any person that will take up and secure the said negroes in any goal, so that I get them again, or will bring them to me, shall receive the above reward, and for either of them one half, and all reasonable expences paid.<BR>ROBERT FREEMAN.<BR>Warren county, June 24.    60
