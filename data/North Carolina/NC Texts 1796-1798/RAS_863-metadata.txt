Slave name(s): Abram
Slaveholder: William Daily
Transcript: FIFTEEN DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RUN away from the subscriber, a Negro Fellow, about twenty years of age, by the name of ABRAM, he had on when he went away, a homespun woolen and thread trowsers, and white course linen shirt, a high crowned wool fur'd hat, half worn, he has two of his upper fore teeth out, by reason of a stroke in fighting, very large feet, hands and wrist, about five feet 8 or 9 inches high; I suppose he has endeavoured for Edenton, as that noted villain Mr. Eagan's Dun, is his father.  Whoever will take up said Fellow and and secure him in gaol, shall receive the above reward, or should they bring him to me at Woodstock, Hyde county, shall receive twenty dollars.<BR>WILLIAM DAILY.<BR>August 25, 1796.<BR>N.B. he run away on Monday the 14th of August.
