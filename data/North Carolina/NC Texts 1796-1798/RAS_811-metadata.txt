Headline: A hundred dollars reward
Publishing newspaper: Fayetteville, N.C. : The North Carolina Minerva and Fayetteville Advertiser
Date of advertisement: 1796-09-24
Page: 3
Time period (decade): 1790-1799
Person placing ad: George Jackson
Date of departure: 1796-09-15
County of residence: Moore County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Simon;Matilda
Slaveholder: George Jackson
Transcript: A HUNDRED DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RUN away from the subscriber on the 15th inst. living in Moore county, five miles from the Court-house, a negro man named SIMON, about 30 years old, 5 feet 6 inches high, dark complexion, rather thin in the face--he can read and write, and no doubt will endeavour to pass for a free man--Likewise run away at the same time, a negro woman named MATILDA: she is about 27 years old. 5 feet high, of a yellow complexion-has a round face and flat nose, is well made and sensible; she was bro't[brought] form the eastern shores of Maryland about two years ago.--Said negroes stole from the subscriber 125l. in gold, and 60 French crowns.-Any person that will deliver me said runaways and money at my plantation, will receive the above reward.<BR> GEO.[GEORGE] JACKSON.<BR>Sept. 15th
