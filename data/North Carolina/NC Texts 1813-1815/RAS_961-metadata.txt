Transcript: Twenty Dollars Reward.<BR>RAN-AWAY from the Subscriber at wheat swamp, Lenoir County, about the first instant, my negro man <BR>CHARLES,<BR>About five feet high, very black, can read and write, and it is probable has a free pass; he can play on the violin; and shews[sic] guilt very remarkable by wrinkeling[sic] his face when accused; he was taken up and put in jail at Newbern about four years ago, it is supposed he is either a lurking about there, or Washington. <BR>The above reward will be given to any person delivering said Negro to me, or lodging him in any jail so that I get him again, and all reasonable expences paid. <BR>All masters of Vessels, and others, are forbid employing said Negro, or carrying him out of the State, under the penalty of the law. <BR>CREED H. DUDLEY.<BR>Lenoir County, April 30, 1814.--278
