Transcript: Twenty Dollars Reward.<BR>RUNAWAY from the subscriber on the 15th November, JERRY a Negro Man about thirty years old, five feet eleven inches high--cues his hair which is 3 or 4 inches long, and has two plaits of [illegible] the same length from the fore part of his head, has lost I think one of his foreteeth.  He was raised in Johnston county, by a Mr. Johnston and sold by him to a Mr. Mish, who sold him to Philanion Hodges, of Cumberland county from whom I purchased him-- Twenty dollars will be given for apprehending and delivering him to me, or ten dollars for securing in any jail so that I get him.<BR>WM.[William] POLK.<BR>Raleigh, Dec. 15, 1813
