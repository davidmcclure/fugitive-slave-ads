Transcript: TEN DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RANAWAY from the Subscriber, living in Rowan county, on the 26th day of July last, a mulatto man named ISAAC, 19 or 20 years old, of the middle size, somewhat stoop shouldered, freckled, in the face and his face is of a reddish cast, and perhaps a small scar in his face occasioned by the kick of a horse, rather a narrow chin, and somewhat wide across his face immediately below his eyes, and very bold in his manner. I will give a reward of Ten Dollars, if the Fellow is delivered to me at my house, in the county of Rowan, eight miles east by south of Salem, or Five Dollars if secured in any jail and notice sent to me so that I get him again.<BR>WOOLRICH RICHARDS.<BR>Rowan, August, 2, 1813. 33 2t
