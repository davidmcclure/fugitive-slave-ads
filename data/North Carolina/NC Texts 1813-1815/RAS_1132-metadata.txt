Transcript: Forty Dollars Reward, <BR>WILL be paid for apprehending and securing in any Jail, so that I get them, two runaway negroes by the name of <BR>BILL and RENTY,<BR>or Twenty Dollars for either on like conditions. These fellow left my plantation, on Cape Fear River in Brunswick County, on the 8th instant, they will either work their way along the sea coast Northwardly, or towards Duplin, Lenoir, or Washington County, whence the old fellow came. <BR>BILL is a stout made, round shouldered, black and plausible fellow, about 50 years old, 5 feet 8 inches high, has a down look when spoken to, can write and read, and may probably forge a pass. <BR>RENTY is about 20 years old, six feet high, very black, has bushy hair and remarkable large feet. Any person apprehending them will please drop a line to me in Brunswick County near Smithfield, with as little delay as possible. <BR>JOHN C. BAKER. <BR>June 20, 1813.--235--3w.
