Transcript: 20 Dollars Reward.<BR>RANAWAY from the subscriber on the 8th. Instant, a Negro man called <BR>DANIEL <BR>He is of a black complexion, low made, about 45 years age, and has a large Ulcer on his head; he carried off a great coat, and wears a handkerchief bound round his head, he was raised in Lenoir County, and is probable lurking about there, or on Neus Road, not far from Newbern.--Also ranaway a few days after, a negro boy called DAVID, about 14 years Old, rather small of his age, black complexion, very sensible and artful, he has been raised as a waiter, and does not like to do any thing else; he came from Virginia, & it is supposed he is about Newbern, or endeavoring to get back there. I will give the above reward for both, or TEN DOLLARS for either if taken out of the Counties of Jones and Craven, or Five Dollars if taken within said Counties, or so secured or delivered to me; and all reasonable expences shall be paid.<BR>JOSEPH HATCH.<BR>Jones County, August 20, 1814.--3w.
