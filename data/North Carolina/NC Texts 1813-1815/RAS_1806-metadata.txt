Time period (decade): 1810-1819

County of residence: Wikes County (N.C.)

Slave name(s): Will
Slaveholder: William Hampton
Transcript: Stop the Runaway.<BR>RAN AWAY from the subscriber living in Wilkes county, on the 18th inst. a mulatto fellow named WILL, 26 or 27 years years[sic] old, of strong muscular form, five feet 7 or 8 inches high, of pleasing countenance, somewhat impertinent in his address, had on when he went off, a brown coloured coat, Suwarrow boots and other clothing, not recollected.  He will no doubt attempt to pass for a free man, and perhaps will procure a pass, or he may pass for a waiting man for one Micajah Lunsford, with whom it is said, he has been in company, he will probably attmept to get to the Ohio state or more probably to Halifax county in this state, or Halifax in Virginia, as it is said he was born in one of those counties.<BR>Whoever will deliver said mullatto to me, or secure him in jail so that I get him again, shall receive a reward of Fifteen Dollars, if taken up within one hundred miles, if beyond that distance Twenty-five dollars.<BR>WILLIAM HAMPTON.<BR>July 25, 1814.    77-4w.
