Transcript: RUNAWAY<BR>ON the 28th of January, 1815, my Negroe [sic] man TOM,  aged about 40 years, rather slender made, his right ear cut close off, and I think has a scar on his forehead, black complected, quite sensible and well tell a good story, he probably has a free pass and likely will try to pass for a freeman and a Preacher--has a yellow wool coat & stripped pantaloons, his other clothes not recollected. It is likely he is in company with a yellow Negroe [sic] with one year off, belonging to George L. Moore. Any person apprehending said Tom and and securing him in Jail so that I get him, shall be entitled to fifteen dollars.<BR>JAS. [JAMES] WALKER.<BR>Orange County, N.C. Feb. 14, 1815.
