Transcript: Twenty Dollars Reward.<BR>RANAWAY from the Subscriber, on the 17th inst living on the Big Allemance, Orange County, two Negro men, one named COFFEN, aged 27 years, slim made 5 feet 6 or 7 inches high, has very long fingers, black complexion; had on striped pantaloons, red and brown worsted vest, old wool and cotton coat, old brown great coat, cut off above the slit, and a new wool hat--the other named JACK, lower than Coffen, but stouter made, about 22 years old, yellow complexion, has a down look, and is a little cross-eyed; had on the same kind of pantaloons and vest coat that Coffen had, and a grey coat, with homemade--a white fur hat, about half worn--Coffen was raised in Suffolk, Virginia, and purchased by John Siler of Chatham, in 1810 of James Matthews of Nansemond county, Va. Jack was raised in Guilford county on Reedyfork. The above reward will be given so that the subscribers get his negroes again or confines them in some jail, or ten dollars for either.<BR>BARNEY CLAPP<BR>July 21<BR>30 3tp
