Transcript: TWENTY DOLLARS REWARD. <BR>RAN-AWAY from the Subscriber, at his plantation at Cedar Grove, Carteret County, about the 20th January, two Negro men viz. <BR>SHARP<BR>is very black, large white eyes, between thirty and thirty-five years old, speaks good english, & is very sensible, is well acquainted with boating & Farming business. <BR>JIM<BR>is about fifty years of age, a surly looking fellow, he has lost some of his toes off one or both of his feet. It is likely they are in Craven County between Newbern and Clubfoots Creek. <BR>I will give the above reward of TWENTY DOLLARS on the delivery of the above named negroes to me, or on their being secured in jail, so that I get them; or Ten Dollars for each. <BR>Masters of vessels, and all others are forwarned from harbouring, employing, or carrying them away under the penalty of the law. <BR>WILLIAM BORDEN. <BR>Cedar Grove, Carteret County, April 30th, 1814.
