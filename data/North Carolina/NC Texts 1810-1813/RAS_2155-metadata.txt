Transcript: RANAWAY<BR>FROM the subscriber on the 12th of August last four Negroes, (to visit,) a Negro man by the name of BOMAN, about five feet three or four inches high, has a fine voice, tolerable dark complected; had on when he went away a cotton shirt and overalls, and a felt hat, but carried sundry clothes with him. Also a Negro woman by the name of PHILLIS, about eighteen years of age, very black and likely; about five feet five or six inches high; wore away a white cotton or copperas frock--The eldest child belonging to said Negro woman that she took with her is about eight years of age,  by the name of CLARRY; she is a likely girl of her age, with tolerable thick lips; has a very noticeable scar on one of her sides, occasioned by a burn; she wore away a white cotton shirt and stripped frock. Also the said Negro woman took with her another child by the name of PATIENCE, about five years of age, a tolerable black child. Any person or persons taking up said Negroes or so apprehending them as I may get them again, or confining them in gaol in this or any state shall have a reward of fiften [sic] dollars. The said Negroes run-away from the subscriber living about 24 miles north of Raleigh, near the Pislidam ford on Neuse River. I purchased the said Negroes of Sterling Johnson, living in Johnston county, 15 miles south of Raleigh, who purchased the two oldest Negroes in the state of Virginia.<BR>HENRY JOHNSON<BR>Wake County, 7th Sept. 1812.
