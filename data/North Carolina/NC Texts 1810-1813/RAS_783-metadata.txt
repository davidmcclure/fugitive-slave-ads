Transcript: 50 Dollars Reward. <BR>RUNAWAY From the Subscriber on the first instant a negro man named <BR>DAVID. <BR>This fellow is about twenty seven or eight years old, five feen ten or eleven inches high, straight, spare and well made, tolerably black, has a handsome face, small hands, and uncommon white teeth; has a small scar on his nose occasioned by a cut with a knife: Is a sensible artful fellow, when speaking fast he Stammers a little and is a tolerable good shoemaker. Had on when he went away a suit of dark grey homsepun cloths. <BR>It is supposed he is at present lurking about on the south side of tar river, near Washington but it is apprehended he will attempt to go out of the State. <BR>The above reward will be paid to any person who will deliver the above named negro to me, or if confined in any jail in the State so that I get him again. <BR>RICHARD GRIST. <BR>Near Washington, N.C. Feb. 4, 1813. <BR>All masters of vessels and others are warned not to harbor, assist, or carry the above fellow out of the State under the rigour of law. <BR>R.G.
