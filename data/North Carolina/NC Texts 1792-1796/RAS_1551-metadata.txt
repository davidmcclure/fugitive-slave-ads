Transcript: RAN away, a Negro fellow named JOE, about five feet four inches high, eighteen or nineteen years old.--He is country born and talks good English.<BR> He ran away from the service of Frederick Foscue, but is now the property of the subscriber.  He is supposed to be lurking about Mr. Foy's,  Mr. Witherspoon's or Swift-creek.<BR>. Whoever will secure him so that his master may get him again, shall be entitled to a reward of FIFTEEN DOLLARS.<BR>Simon Foscue.<BR>Jones County, March 12.
