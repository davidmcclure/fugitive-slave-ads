Transcript: TWENTY DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RAN AWAY from the subscriber about the 15th of October last, a mulatto fellow named GEORGE, about 30 years old, of a low stature and well built, of affable speech and pleasant countenance.  It is expected he may occasionally take a tour to Martin county, about Welch's Creek, to Captain Francis Pugh's in Bertie, where he has some relations, or to the head of Hoskey Swamp, in Hertford county, at all which places he has been heard of.  Whoever apprehends said Negro, and will deliver him to the subscriber, shall receive the above reward, and all reasonable expences.<BR>BENJAMIN WILLIAMS.<BR>Cashie-Neck, April 7, 1796.   1m.
