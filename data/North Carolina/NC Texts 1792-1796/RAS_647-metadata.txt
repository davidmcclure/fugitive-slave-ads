Headline: Twenty dollars reward
Publishing newspaper: Edenton, N.C. : State Gazette of North Carolina
Date of advertisement: 1795-10-08
Page: 3
Time period (decade): 1790-1799
Person placing ad: Asa Lee
Date of departure: 1795-07-17
County of residence: Currituck County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): But Easter;Tom
Slaveholder: Asa Lee
Transcript: TWENTY DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RUN away from the subscriber on the 17th July last, two Negro Men Slaves, of a black complexion; one of them, named BUT EASTER, is a good seaman, can speak some broken French, is apt to drink hard, has a lump on one of his shoulders, and is branded thereon, supposed with a W.  He is an artful active fellow, can read print a little, and may have a free pass; he is stout and well made.  The other is nearly the same height, but not so well made, and is named TOM.  The above reward will be given to any person apprehending and securing them so that I get them, and all necessary expences paid.<BR>ASA LEE.<BR>Currituck, August 11, 1795.
