Transcript: 20 dollars reward.<BR>RUNAWAY from the subscriber on the first inst. in Tyrrel County a negro man named<BR>ANTHONY;<BR>he is about twenty two years old; of a dark complection; stout made; about five feet six or seven inches high--when he went away, he wore white duck trowsers and a gray woolen jacket, lined with red flannel; but as he carried other cloaths[sic] with him, he will no doubt change his dress. He may be lurking about Lee's Mills in Washington County, or about Miss Rhode's negro Silvy's near Washington, where he has a wife. The above reward will be given to any person who will apprehend and lodge him in Washington County goal, or Beaufort County goal, so that I get him again.<BR>All Masters of vessels are forwarned from taking him on board their vessels, under the penalty of the law.<BR>JESSE D. CARRAWAY.<BR>Washington, Oct. 8, 1819--tf227
