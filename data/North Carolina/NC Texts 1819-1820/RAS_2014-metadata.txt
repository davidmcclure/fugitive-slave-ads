Transcript: 20 Dollars Reward.<BR>WILL be given for apprehending and securing in some jail so that I get him again, Negro<BR>GEORGE,<BR>a mulatto 19 years of age, intelligent and well made, genteel deportment and quick action, has been bred to the business of house servant, was formerly the property of Samuel Chesson, Jr. of Washington County. George has made several attempts to escape to Philadelphia, where he says he will be free, for which purpose I attempted to brand him with the letter R on each cheek, which may be identified by a near examination. He went off to company with a black boy, whose aim is Philadelphia, and it is presumed they will go by the way of Washington County, and from thence to Norfolk. George absconded on the 13th inst.<BR>Peter Evans.<BR>Tarborough, May 23, 1820.
