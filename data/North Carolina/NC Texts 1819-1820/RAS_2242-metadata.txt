Transcript: FIFTY DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RUNAWAY from the subscriber, on the Sunday night last, two negroes, Virgil and Guilford--Virgil is a sout built likely fellow, between thirty and thirty-five years of age, of ordinary complection for a negro, supposed to be about five feet two or three inches high, speaks quick when spoken to, a little bow legged, and has some knowledge of the Blacksmith's trade. Guilford a boy eighteen or nineteen years of age, yellow complection, well grown, stammers in his speech, and more particularly so when embarrassed or examined,  and a little knock need. Their clothing is not recollected--Said negroes it is though have carried off with them, a negro girl from the said neighborhood by the name Gilly, or otherwise carried away by some white incendiary, either to the western or southern states. Any person who will deliver said negroes to me or either of them, shall receive 25 dollars for each of said boys, or if they shall be confined in any jail so that I get them again, or the above reward for them both.<BR>JAS.[James] C. JONES.<BR>Portridge, Franklin County, August 18, 1819.
