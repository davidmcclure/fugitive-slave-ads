Transcript: $25 REWARD.<BR>RAN away form[sic] the subscriber, living in Lenoir County, on Saturday, the 23d inst. a Negro man named JERRY, about 23 years of age, near six feet high, slender made, and has a light complexion.  He formerly belonged to John R. Donnell, Esq. who sold him to Lazarus Smith, of Wayne County.  It is supposed he is lurking about Newbern, or Slocumb's Creek, having relations at both places.<BR>A reward of Twenty-five Dollars will be given to any person who will apprehend and deliver said fellow to the subscriber, or secure him in any jail so that he gets him again.<BR>Any person wishing to purchase him, can do so, by paying 825 dollars.<BR>JOHN GRADY.<BR>Lenoir, Jan. 30, 1819.--2w*
