Transcript: $100 REWARD.<BR>RANAWAY from the Subscribers, about the 20th April last, the following Slaves belonging to the Estate of the late James Beggs, of Fayetteville:<BR>FEBABY, a bright Mulatto woman, aged 38 years, small of stature and cross eyed,--and her three children, John, Henry and Ann Eliza.<BR>JOHN is about 21 years of age, a Blacksmith by trade, a bright mulatto, slender make, and somewhat blear-eyed.<BR>HENRY is about 18 years, light colour, and has also worked at the Blacksmith's trade.<BR>ANN ELIZA, a bright Mulatto, aged 16, and not well grown.<BR>There is reason to believe that the said Slaves have been assisted in making their escape, as Henry has been seen in the City of New York.--The above reward will be paid for their delivery to the Subscribers, or a proportional part for either of them.<BR>DAVID TORRANCE,<BR>HUGH McLAURIN,<BR>Ex'rs.[Executors] of James Beggs.<BR>August 28, 1838.<BR>8tf
