Transcript: Ran Away<BR>From the Point Peter Plantation of the subscriber,<BR>three NEGRO MEN,<BR>ISAAC, CAESAR, AND GILBERT.<BR>GILBERT is five feet five inches, twenty years old, thick set, full face, small eyes. ISAAC, aged thirty or thirty five years, is nearly six feet, well made, light complexioned, laughs when spoken to. He is probably in Sambo Neck, or he may be gone to Bladen county. CAESAR is about five feet and a half, very bowlegged, light complexioned, aged about thirty five, with a blemish in one of his eyes. He is well known about town and its vicinity. he has a brother working on the jettee, and an uncle, called Abram De Rossett, lives in a new house, near the new Episcopal Church, where I found his clothes. Caesar is well acquainted with all sorts of vessel work, and will probably try to get work on board of vessels lying at the lower steam mills.<BR>A REWARD of 45 Dollars will be paid for the delivery of the three to S.R. POTTER, at the plantation, or lodged in the jail of New Hanover county; or fifteen dollars for either.<BR>All masters of vessels are warned not to harbour, or carry off said negroes, or either of them.<BR>SAMUEL R. POTTER<BR>Wilmington, May 18th, 1838.<BR>123[?] tf
