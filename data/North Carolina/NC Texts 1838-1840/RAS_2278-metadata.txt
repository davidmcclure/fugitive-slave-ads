Transcript: Ran Away<BR>FROM the Point Peter Plantation of the subscriber three NEGRO MEN,<BR>ISAAC, PETER, AND GILBERT.<BR>ISAAC is a stout built fellow, of about 5 feet 10 inches, not very black, speaks slow and distinctly, with a downward look, 30 or 35 years of age.<BR>PETER is a slender, tall mulatto, about 20 years old, and talks well and distinctly.<BR>GILBERT is a small jet black fellow, about 5 feet 8 inches, 20 years of age, and speaks plain and distinctly.<BR>Isaac absconded about the middle of April, and may be lurking in the vicinity of Sambo Neck, as his friends belonged to the Waddell estate. Peter and Gilbert ran away this morning, and are probably in the vicinity of Wilmington. A REWARD of $45 will be paid for the delivery of the three to S. R. Potter at the plantation, or lodged in the jail of New Hanover county; or fifteen dollars for either.<BR>All masters of vessels are warned not to harbour, or carry off said negroes, or either of them.<BR>SAMUEL R. POTTER.<BR>Wilmington, May 8th, 1838.<BR>181 tf
