Headline: Twenty dollars reward
Publishing newspaper: Edenton, N.C. : The Edenton Gazette and North Carolina General Advertiser
Date of advertisement: 1817-10-07
Page: 4
Time period (decade): 1810-1819
Person placing ad: John Worrel
Date of departure: 1817-09-14
County of residence: Gates County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Jacob
Slaveholder: John Worrel
Transcript: Twenty Dollars Reward.<BR>RUNAWAY from the subscriber on Wednesday, the 14th inst. Negro man JACOB, Twenty-one years of age, five feet eight or nine inches high, slender made, of a yellowish complexion; had on when he absconded a short Jacket and Pantaloons of mixed home made cloth.--It is expected he will endeavor to make his escape to the North in some vessel. Any Person who will apprehend and deliver said Negro Jacob to me, or secure him in Jail, so that I get him again, shall receive the above reward. All masters of vessels and others are cautioned against harbouring, employing or carrying him off.<BR>John Worrel.<BR>Gates County, 12th Sept. 1817.
