Slave name(s): Sarah;Tilly;Ascension
Slaveholder: Thomas Howenton, deceased
Transcript: TWENTY-FIVE DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>STOLEN or Runaway the 30t of last month FOUR NEGROES  belonging to the Estate of Thomas Howenton, dec'd[deceased] viz:--SARAH and three children, Sarah, Tilly, and Ascension, the woman is about 35 years of age, Sarah (the child) 8 or 10, Tilly  6 or 7  and Ancension 2 or three years of age, all tolerable black, the above reward will be given for apprehending and securing said Negroes so that I get them again. <BR>  It is believed the negroes were stolen by a man named Joseph Horn,  who lives not far from Fort Claiborne, Alabama Territory, he had set up a claim to them & had been in the neighbourhood for some weeks previous to the negroes disappearing: said Horn is between 30 and 35 years of age, spare made and wore large whiskers which nearly met on his chin.<BR>If stolen $50 will be given for the apprehension and conviction of the thief.<BR>Letters on the subject to be addressed to me at Enfield.<BR>J. BENTON, Act'g. Exr.<BR>Edgecomb county, Dec. 15th, 1818. 51-5w.
