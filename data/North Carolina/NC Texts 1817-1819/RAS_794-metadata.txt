Date of advertisement: 1818-05-09

Time period (decade): 1810-1819

Date of departure: 1818-04-20
Presumed or intended destination: Mr. Fonville's
Slave name(s): Harry
Slaveholder: John Bonner
Transcript: 50 DOLLARS REWARD<BR>RANAWAY From the Subscriber, on Monday, 20th of April, his negro man<BR>HARRY.<BR>He is about five feet five or six inches high, very dark complected, and a stout well made fellow--had on when he absconded, a home made suit of clothes, and carried with him two short coats, blue and grey.  The said Fellow has been hired by Mr. George Lane of Craven County, for one or two years, and ranaway the whole or greater part of the time, he has a wife at Mr. Fonville's, about fourteen miles above Newbern, where it is probable he will be lurking and where he is generally known.  The above reward will be given to any person that will apprehend said fellow, and secure him in either of the Jails of Newbern or Washington, so that I get him again.<BR>All persons are cautioned not to harbour, employ, or carry him off, under penalty of the law.<BR>JOHN BONNER.<BR>Fair-field, near Washington, April 23d, 1818---eow6t.
