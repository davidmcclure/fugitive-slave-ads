Headline: 10 dollars reward.  Ran-Away
Publishing newspaper: New Bern, N.C. : Carolina Federal Republican
Date of advertisement: 1818-03-28
Page: 3
Time period (decade): 1810-1819
Person placing ad: Joseph Hatch
Date of departure: 1818-03-18
County of residence: Jones  County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Amy
Slaveholder: Joseph Hatch
Transcript: 10 DOLLARS REWARD.<BR>RAN-AWAY<BR>FROM the Subscriber living in Jones County, on the 18th inst. a negro woman by the name of<BR>AMY,<BR>she is tall and stout built, yellow complexion, about 40 years of age, with several scars on her cheek and back of her neck, walks wih her toes very much out, and has a very sullen look. The above negro woman was taken up last winter by Mr. Guilford D. Murphy, up Neuse, where she was harboured.--She says she was harboured by a fellow by the name of Sam, belonging to Gen. Thomas A. Green, she then went to Gen. Simpson's Plantation, where she was harboured by his Negroes; then crossed over Neuse and was harboured by Mr. Patricks and Wm.[William] Bryans negroes.--I will give the above reward for her, if delivered to me or secured in Jail so that I get her.<BR>Masters of vessels and all others are forbid harbouring, employing or carrying her away under the penalty of the law.<BR>Joseph Hatch.<BR>March 28th, 1818.--3t.
