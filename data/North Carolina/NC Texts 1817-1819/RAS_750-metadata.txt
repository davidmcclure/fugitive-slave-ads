Transcript: 40 DOLLARS REWARD. <BR>RAN-AWAY from the Subscriber on the night of the 10th inst. two Negroes, <BR>ABRAM and PEG. <BR>ABRAM is a stout well made fellow, yellow complexion, about 35 years of age, nearly six feet high, has lost some of his fore teeth, & is branded with the letters E. H. on his check --PEG the wife of ABRAM, is about 25 years of age, yellow complexion, spare made, 5 feet 3 or 4 inches high and has a squint eye.--The above negroes had on when they left my Plantation Cotton homespun Clothes, and I expect they are lurking in Craven County on Neuse, where they have a number of connexions[sic].--I will give the above reward of FORTY DOLLARS, to any person who will apprehend said negroes, and secure them in Newbern Jail, or deliver them to me at my Plantation in Jones County, and all reasonable expences paid. <BR>Buckner Hatch. <BR>Jones County, Nov. 14, 1817.
