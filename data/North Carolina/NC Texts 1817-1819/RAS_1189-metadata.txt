Page: 2
Time period (decade): 1810-1819

County of residence: Craven County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Joe; John; America; Andrew; Charles; Kit; Ben; Suck
Slaveholder: Edmund Hatch
Transcript: NOTICE.<BR>RANAWAY From the Subscriber's residence, three miles above Newbern on Trent road, the five following described negroes, viz.: JOE, a stout made fellow, nearly six feet high, of a yellowish complexion, a little knock kneed,  walks very wide, and has lost some of his toes by the frost.  JOHN a low well built fellow, of a yellowish complexion, has swelled eyes, and is branded on each cheek with the letters E.H. His upper fore teeth are rotten, and when spoken to, is apt to give rather an impudent answer.  AMERICA, a low built woman, has thick lips, and when spoken to, appears to be rather embarassed.<BR>ANDREW, a yellow fellow, stout made, with very wide fore teeth, a large round face, and has a large scar on his left foot, occasioned by the cut of an axe.  CHARLES, a black fellow, very knock-need[sic], slim and crooked, with large wide feet ; he is about five feet ten inches high and swings himself when walking, something like a seaman. KIT, a yellow fellow, about 36 years of age, 5 feet 6 or 7 inches high, with large freckles his fore teeth wide apart, and is a carpenter by trade. BEN, a light complected black fellow about 38 or 39 years of age, 5 feet 6 or 7 inches high, with very large feet and hands, thick hair, and teeth wide apart, when speaking, her turns up his upper lip, and has a large scar across his nose.  SUCK, a tall negro wench, 21 or 22 years of age, light complexion, thin visage, large full eyes, and some of her fore teeth rotten.<BR>Six hundred dollars will be given for Ben, Kit, John and Joe, or one fourth of that sum for each or either of them. Four hundred dollars will be given for American Andrew Charles and Suck, or one fourth of that sum for each or either of them.<BR>All manner of persons are forwarned from harbouring or carrying away any of the above named negroes, under the penalty of the law.<BR>EDMUND HATCH.<BR>Craven County, July 11th. 1818--17,tf
