Transcript: NOTICE<BR>RANAWAY From the Subscriber's residence, three miles above Newbern on Trent road, the five following described negroes, viz. JOE, a stout made fellow, nearly six feet high, of a yellowish complexion, a little knock kneed, walks very wide, and has lost some of his toes by the frost.  JOHN a low well built fellow, of a yellowish complexion, has swelled eyes, and is branded on each cheek with the letters E.H.  His upper fore teeth are rotten, and when spoken to, is apt to give rather an impudent answer.  AMERICA, a low built woman, has thick lips, and when spoken to, appears to be rather embarrassed.  ANDREW, a yellow fellow, stout made, with very wide fore teeth, a large round face, and has a large scar on his left foot, occasioned by the cut of an axe.  CHARLES, a black fellow, very knock-need[sic], slim and crooked, with large wide feet; he is about five feet ten inches high and swings himself when walking something like a seaman.<BR>One hundred dollars will be given for JOE and JOHN, or Fifty for either of them; thirty dollars for America; fifty for Andrew and Charles, or twenty-five for either of them; if delivered to me or secured so that I get them.--All reasonable expences will be paid.<BR>All manner of persons are forwarned from harbouring or carrying away any of the above named negroes, under the penalty of the law.<BR>EDMUND HATCH.<BR>Craven County, June 10th. 1818-13,tf.
