Slaveholder: Thomas Graham

Transcript: Ten Dollars Reward.<BR>RANAWAY<BR>From the Subscriber on Wednesday the 5th Instant,<BR>A NEGRO MAN named SAM about 5 Feet 10 Inches high, & about 34 Years old : rather between a yellowish and a black Complexion, with remarkable small legs.  He took with him a short blue Cloth Coat without Skirts, a sand coloured long coat, and a light coloured Cloth Curtout, also a light mixt Homsepun Coat, he took with him also a green Waistcoat with long wool, or Lion's Skim, with sundry other Clothes in a Knapsack on his Back.  I expect he will aim to go to Grassy Creek, in Granville County, near the Virginia line, as I purchased him of a Mr. Thomas Kilbreath of that County.<BR>Any Person who will apprehend said Negro and secure him in any Goal so that I get him again shall receive the above Reward, and all reasonable Charges paid by me.<BR>THOMAS GRAHAM.<BR>Stokes County, N.C.<BR>March 13th, 1806}
