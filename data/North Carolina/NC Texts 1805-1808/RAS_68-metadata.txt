Time period (decade): 1800-1809
Person placing ad: Thomas Clegg
Date of departure: 1805-12-26
County of residence: Chatham County (N.C.)
Presumed or intended destination: Virginia
Slave name(s): Abner
Slave alias: Levin
Slaveholder: James Polk
Transcript: RAN AWAY,<BR>From the Subscriber's house in Chatham county, near Pittsborough, on the 26[t]h December, 1805, A NEGRO MAN by the name of Abner, and is often called Levin, about twenty-two years old, stout bulk, with a large scar on the right side of his face, with his right eye out, occasioned by a kick from a horse when a boy, had on when he went away, a sailor's Jacket of light brown stuff (called lion's skin) lined with yellow flannel, with metal buttons, and a pair of cloth trowsers, of grey colour.  The said Negro is the Property of James Polk, late from Virginia: It is expected he will make towards that State.  Whoever will secure the said Negro so that the owner may get him, or give information to the Subscriber, shall be handsomely rewarded.<BR>THOMAS CLEGG.
