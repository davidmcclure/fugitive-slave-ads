Headline: Twenty-five dollars reward

Date of advertisement: 1806-04-14
Page: 3
Time period (decade): 1800-1809
Person placing ad: Joseph Boon
County of residence: Johnston County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Sam;Harry
Slaveholder: Joseph Boon
Former slaveholder: Col. Joseph Green;Richard Warren
Transcript: Twenty five Dollars Reward.<BR>RANAWAY from my Plantation on Neuse River, in Johnston County, two Negro Fellows, named Sam and Harry.  Sam formerly belonged to Col. Joseph Green, he is yellow complected, thin Face, and speaks hoarse, he is about Twenty-five Years old, five feet nine or ten inches high.  his dress when he went off consisted of grey Plains.  Harry formerly belonged to Richard Warren of Johnston County; he is very black and has a sullen look out of his eyes, the white of them are inclined to be red; he is about Eighteen or Nineteen Years old, five Feet seven or eight Inches high.  His Dress when he went off I cannot fairly ascertain, he had blue Overalls on and perhaps a cotton Shirt.  I do not recollect any particular marks about him.<BR>Whoever will apprehend said Negroes and bring them to me, shall be entitled to the above Reward--If out of this State, a Reward of Fifty Dollars will be given, if delivered to me in North-Carolina, or will confine them in any Jail so that I get them, shall be handsomely rewarded.<BR>It is supposed they will aim for Tar River.<BR>JOSEPH BOON, jun.<BR>Apr 14, 1806
