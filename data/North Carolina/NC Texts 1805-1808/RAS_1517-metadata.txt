Transcript: RAN away from the subscriber on the 18th instant, two Negro men, each of them between the age of twenty and thirty; one by the name of PETER, stout and well made, about five feet ten or eleven inches high, has a long rolling carrige[sic] in his walk, carried with him four shirts, one of fine linen with broad risbands[sic] and collar, two pair of homespun overalls, a pair of country made shoe boots; the other fellow short and well made, about five feet five inches high, by the name of JOSH, had with him two homepsun shirts, two or three pair of overalls, one of which was striped cassimere, and a striped Horseman's jacket, homespun made.<BR>Any person apprehending and securing the said Runaway in any jail in this District, or any other in the state, shall receive a reward of Twenty Dollars.<BR>FREDRICK SIMPSON<BR>N.B. All masters of vessels are cautioned against harbouring or employing said negroes under the penalty of the law. It is supposed they will aim for the state of Virginia or Maryland.<BR>July 22, 1806.
