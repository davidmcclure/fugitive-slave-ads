Transcript: 10 DOLLARS REWARD<BR>RAN-AWAY from the subscriber, living near Hillsborough, on the night of the 24th instant, a Negro man by the name of WILSON, the property of Willis Randall of the state of Georgia, which negro was left in my care.  He is between 25 and 30 years of age, about 5 feet 5 or 6 inches high, stout made, of a yellow complection, a scar over his left eye, & a vacancy in his foreteeth in the under jaw, had on a pair of country cloth overalls, striped up and down with two threads of blue yarn and two threads of copperas cotton, an old blue broad cloth coat, a tolerable good hat, a striped silk handkerchief, and is thought to have taken a pitching axe with him.  He was bought in Norfolk last winter by said Randall it is supposed he will make for that place.  Any person taking and securing said Negro in any jail so that the owner may get him again, shall receive the above reward by me.<BR>WILLIAM CARINGTON.<BR>May 27, 1807.    3w
