Transcript: Wilmington, N.C. 15th Dec. 1807<BR>RAN-AWAY from the subscriber on Saturday last, the 12th inst. a negro man named Moses.<BR>He is stout & well made, about 30 years of age--had on when he went away a blue jacket and trousers, with a pair half boots much worn; he speaks plain and can tell a plausible story; he formerly belonged to Mr. Wm.[William] Rea or Kea of Bladen County and well is acquainted about Gen. Brown's and David Gillespie's on the North-West.<BR>Ten Dollars Reward and all travelling expences, will be paid to any person that will secure him in Wilmington goal or on delivering him to the subscriber.<BR>N.B. All persons are forbid employing him, or masters of vessels carrying him off, as the law will be rigidly enforced against them.    2w.
