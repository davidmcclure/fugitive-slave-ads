Headline: Run away
Publishing newspaper: Raleigh, N.C. : Raleigh Register and North Carolina Weekly Advertiser
Date of advertisement: 1806-09-29
Page: 3
Time period (decade): 1800-1809
Person placing ad: Richard Wood
County of residence: Granville County (N.C.)
Slave name(s): Obadiah
Slaveholder: Richard Wood
Transcript: RUN AWAY,<BR>Or conveyed away by some person, from the subscriber, living in the county of Granville, on the waters of Tar River,<BR>A Likely young NEGRO FELLOW, by the name of Obadiah, between 21 and 22 years of age, thin visage, and generally slow spoken, of dark complexion, with the following marks, viz. a lump on the back of his neck near the size of a Partridge egg : just above the wrist on his right arm, there is a lump from a stroke, and the middle finger on his left hand is crooked, occasioned by the cut of a knife.  Had on when he went away, a pair of cotton trowsers[sic], thin shirt, a coat twisted with black and white lap.  Took off with him, two pair of buff cassimere breeches, two waistcoats, one white cotton, the other grey broadcloth : one pair of white wool stockings ribbed, and one broadcloth coat of dark grey.  Any person that will bring me the said Negro Man, or give intelligence so that I get him, shall receive the following Reward from the subscriber: Five Dollars, if taken within ten miles; and Ten Dollars for any distance above; if conveyed awyay, Fifty Dollars for the Thief.<BR>RICHARD WOOD.<BR>Sept. 12
