Transcript: NOTICE.<BR>RUN away from the subscriber's plantation near Webb's Ferry, Anson County, on the 21st instant, four likely African Negro men, three of which are of a yellow complexion marked on the face and body with their Country marks; the other a very black fellow, marked only on his body, and is named JACK-the other three named AKA, SAM and BOATSWAIN: They had on when they went away, checked shirts and white homespun trowsers [sic]: they speak no English. It is supposed they crossed the river Pedee, and are now on the East side. Whoever will deliver the said Negroes to the subscriber, or shall be generously rewarded and expences paid.<BR>J. LANIER.<BR>September 28th, 1807.
