Transcript: Absconded,<BR>FROM the employ of the Cape-Fear-Navigation Company, a Negro man slave named JERRY, formerly the property of Lovet Bell, of Hyde county, by whom he was raised, and sold, about 6 months ago, to Stephen Owens and Samuel Vines, of Beaufort county, from whom the company purchased. Jerry is about 6 feet high, very likely and well made; yellow complexion ; supposed to have taken with him jacket, trowsers[sic] and pea jacket of blue Plains, shirt of red flannel, and, in other respects, well clothed.<BR>ALSO:<BR>A Negro man slave named BEN, raised and owned by Betsey Bonner of Beaufort county, near Washington--sold by her to Thomas Trotten and by him to the said Stephen Owens and Samuel Vines from whom the Company purchased. Ben is about 5 feet 10 inches high, of black complexion, aged 28 years, and clothed in the same manner as Jerry.<BR>For apprehension and delivery to the subscriber, in Fayetteville of either of the above slaves, Ten Dollars reward will be paid if taken within the county of Cumberland and Twenty Dollars if without it. <BR>JOHN WINSLOW, President<BR>Cape-Fear Navigation Company<BR>February 18      35-tf
