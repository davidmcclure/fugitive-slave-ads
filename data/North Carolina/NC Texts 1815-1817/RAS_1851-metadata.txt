Transcript: TEN DOLLARS REWARD. <BR>RAN AWAY from Roger Jones' Plantation on Beards Creek, Craven County, about the last of February, a negro fellow by the name of MARCUS, forty years of age, black complexion, about five feet high and speaks very bold. He was under the control of the said Jones, but the Subscriber has since hired him, and will give the above reward to any person who will deliver him to me, or confine him so thta[that] I get him, and all reasonable expences will be paid. <BR>WM.[WILLIAM] C. NELSON. <BR>Adams' Creek, Craven County,<BR>April 1st, 1815.
