Headline: Fifty dollars reward
Publishing newspaper: Wilmington, N.C. : Wilmington Gazette
Date of advertisement: 1816-04-06
Page: 4
Time period (decade): 1810-1819
Person placing ad: John London
Date of departure: 1816-02
Slave name(s): Billy
Slaveholder: John London
Transcript: Fifty Dollars Reward.<BR>RUNAWAY from the subscriber a Mulatto Man Servant named<BR>BILLY,<BR>about 26 years of age, 5 feet 6 or 7 inches high, well made and stammers in his speech when the least hurried--he had on when he went off, a blue jacket and overhauls with white metal buttons; he took with him other clothes, particularly a blue boat and pantaloons almost new, two white shirts, one marked with his name, and a hat which has been worn with powder, also sundry bedding, among which a new duffell blanket. It is apprehended, he will endeavour to get off in some vessel, having once before [illegible] reward will be paid if found on board any vessel, on delivery of him to the subscriber, or lodging him in the jail of Wilmington. And twenty five dollars if apprehended on shore on delivery as aforesaid.<BR>All masters of vessels or other persons, are warned against carrying away the above named Billy, or in anywise harbouring, concealing or employing the said fellow, as I am determined to prosecute with the utmost rigour of the law any person who so offend. He went off late on Friday night last or early on Saturday morning, and without any previous provocation.<BR>John London.<BR> He has a wife and family at major Moors plantation at Force Put and a mother and other connections at the plantation of S. Black, Esq. near Hollyshelter.<BR> February 24.
