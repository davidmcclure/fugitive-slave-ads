Transcript: Sixty Dollars REWARD. <BR>RAN AWAY from the Subscriber, at his Plantation, on the first of April, two Negro Men, DANIEL & CAEZAR. Daniel is a low well made fellow, black complexion, was raised up Neuse at the Plantation of Mr. William Blackledge, above Cox's ferry, has a brother (a Pilot) at Ocracock, by the name of Cain; it is likely he will be lurking about one or both of those places. I will give a reward of TEN DOLLARS for said fellow if delivered to me or secured in Jail so that I get him. <BR>CAEZAR is a tall black fellow, has a wife by the name of Dinah, belonging to Mr. John Frazier, in Newbern, and no doubt he will be lurking about there or on Trent and White Oak rivers. I will give a reward of EIGHTY DOLLARS for the above named Negro Caezar, if delivered to me or secured in Jail so that I get him. <BR>Said fellow is legally outlawed. <BR>JOSEPH HATCH. <BR>Jones County, June 20, 1816. <BR>3t91.
