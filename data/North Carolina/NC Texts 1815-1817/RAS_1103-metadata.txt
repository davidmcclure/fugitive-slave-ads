Transcript: Fifty Dollars REWARD. <BR>RAN AWAY from the Subscriber's Plantation on Wheat Swamp, Greene County, in the month of September last, a Negro fellow by the name of<BR>CAEZAR. <BR>about seventeen years of age, 4 feet 8 inches high, thick set and well grown, full eyes and yellow complexion; had on when he went away, a new homespun Jacket Trowsers, (Copperas warp.) It is supposed he is lurking about Newbern, and am told has altered his dress, and wears a tarred hat, and it is believed that he will try to get on board of some vessel and make his escape out of the State. All persons are forwarned from harboring, employing or carrying him out of the State, under the penalty of the law. <BR>I will give the above reward for him to be delivered to me, or secure him in Jail so that I get him, and all reasoanble expences shall be paid. <BR>SAMUEL MOORE, Jun. <BR>Greene County, June 15, 1816. <BR>2m390.
