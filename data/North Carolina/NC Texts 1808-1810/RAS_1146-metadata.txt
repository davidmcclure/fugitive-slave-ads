Transcript: IF WITHIN FOUR WEEKS, <BR> Ten DOLLARS reward will be given for apprehending, and delivering to the subscriber in Edenton, a negro man named HARRY, belonging to the estate of Richard Benbury, dec'd[deceased]. He is of middle stature, black, rather of an abject countenance and slow in speech-pretends to be a carpenter, and is extremely indolent--was thinly clad when he absconded. It is presented he is either in Edenton where his wife resides or its vicinity where his father and mother belong, or in some of the lower counties, where, in his master's life time, he attempted to seek a lurking place. Should he be apprehended out of Chowan county all reasonable expences will be likewise paid. <BR> Jos.[Joseph] B. Skinner. <BR>Edenton Jan. 16, 1809. 4w 152
