Former slaveholder: General J.A. Pearson
Transcript: RAN AWAY. <BR> FROM the subscriber living in the county of Rowan, on the 24th June, a negro man named Davy, this boy formerly belonged to Gen. J.A. Pearson, dec'd[deceased]; he is well known as his carriage driver and servant. He is tall in stature, of very black complexion, about twenty-eight years of age, his clothing not recollected. I have no idea where he is gone. Any person giving information to the subscriber, at Mock's Old Field, or securing him in jail, or otherwise, so as he may be again in my possession, shall be liberally rewarded.<BR> E.M. PEARSON.<BR> June, 25, 1823.
