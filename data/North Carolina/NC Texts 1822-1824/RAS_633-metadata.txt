Transcript: Twenty Dollars Reward.<BR>RANAWAY from the Subscriber, living in Fayetteville, N.C. about the month of January, 1822, a Negro Man by the name of<BR>REDDOCK.<BR>25 or 30 years of age, 5 feet 8 or 10 inches high, slender made, bright complexion, pleasing countenance, and has tolerable thicks[sic] lips. Reddock was purchased about three years ago of Mr. Brittain H Saunders, who then lived about 12 miles south of Raleigh, on the Stage Road leading to this place; has been hired in the neighborhood of Mrs. Banks, 10 miles south of Raleigh, where he has a wife and I have good reason to believe, that he is lurking about that neighborhood.<BR>A reward of 15 Dollars will be given to any person who will lodge the negro in any jail, so that I get him again, or twenty Dollars, if delivered to me in Fayetteville.<BR>J. A. Mumford.<BR>Feb 27    39:2w.
