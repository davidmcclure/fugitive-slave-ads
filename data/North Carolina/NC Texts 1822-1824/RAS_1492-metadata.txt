Transcript: One Hundred Dollars<BR>REWARD<BR>RAN AWAY from the Subscriber, on Saturday last, three Negro Men, viz.<BR>Cuff, David and Moses.<BR>CUFF is about twenty-seven years of age--five feet seven or eight inches high, tolerably stout, and well set--his complexion rather dark.<BR>DAVID is about twenty-five years old--nearly six feet high, slender make, and his complexion something lighter than Cuff's.<BR>MOSES is about five feet ten inches high, stout and well made, and of a dark complexion.<BR>The above reward of One Hundred Dollars will be given to any person that will apprehend the said Negroes, and confine them in any Jail so that I get them--or a proportional part of said sum will be given for either of them.<BR>It is supposed that they have joined company with some other negroes that ranaway from this neighbourhood at the same time, and that they will endeavor to escape into some of the Northern States.<BR>All persons are forwarned from harbouring, and Masters of vessels from carrying them away, under the penalty of the law.<BR>Reuben Dennis.<BR>Carteret County, near Beaufort, N.C.<BR>July 9th, 1822--'26 4
